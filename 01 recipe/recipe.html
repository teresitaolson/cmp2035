Mom's Granola

Adapted from The Flour Cookbook by Joanne Chang

This clasic granola recipe is full of hearty, earthy, nutty flavors and is simply adorned with a handful of dried cranberries. Better yet, this granola recipe is fool proof! Itâ€™s warm and nutty; perfect for a satisfying snack or breakfast. There are many ways to enjoy this treat - as a partner for yogurt, as a crunchy side-kick to fruit, or as a perfect topping for ice cream. Mostly, I like to eat it by the handful as a slightly sweet snack for a late afternoon pick me up.

Ingredients

    2-1/2 cups (270 grams )old-fashioned rolled oats (not instant or quick cooking
    1-1/2 cups (270 grams) wheat germ
    1/2 cup (60 grams) sweetened shredded coconut
    3 tablespoons seasame seeds
    1/3 cup (40 grams)sunflower seeds
    1/2 cup (50 grams)chopped walnuts
    1/2 cup (50 grams) sliced almonds
    2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
    1 teaspoon kosher salt
    3/4 cup (255 grams) honey
    2 teaspoons vanilla extract
    1/2 cup (80 grams)dried cranberries (optional)

Makes about 10 cups

Directions

  1. Position a rack in the center of the oven, and heat the oven to 350 degrees F. Line the baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Combine the oats, wheat germ, coconut, seasame seeds, sunflower seeds, walnuts, almonds, cinnamon, and salt in a large bowl. Mix well.
  3. Whisk together the oil, honey and vanilla in a seperate small bowl.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients over the dry ingredients and mix thoroughly until the dry ingredients are evenly coated.
  5. Pour the mixture onto the prepared baking sheet and spread in an even layer.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until golden brown.
  7. Stir the granola with a spatula or wooden spoon several times during baking to ensure even browning. Let cool immediately, then add the cranberries and mix to distribute them evenly.

Note:

The granola can be stored up in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 week.

Optional Add-ons

You can replace cranberries with 1/2 cup of dried fruit of your choice (raisins, dried cherries or cacao nibs).
