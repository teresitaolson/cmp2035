Project: TYPE DESIGNER EXHIBIT

Atomized History -  A retrospective collection of Tobias Frere-JonesTobias Frere-Jones is a creator of over five hundred fonts and counting. In this project, the goal is to create an exhibit identity for the featured artist.  The title, Atomized History, describes Frere Jones’s design approach where the design problem is divided into parts and looking at different historical periods for pieces of the solution.  Type images from New York City served as inspiration to capture the spirit of the exhibition.

Project: SOCIAL CAUSE POSTER

DEAR FUTURE SELF poster series - Each year, nearly 72,000 adolescents and young adults ages 15-39 are diagnosed with cancer. Fertility preservation is a highly important survivorship issue among this population and numerous factors continue to hamper its routine integration into clinical practice.The goal of the Future Self poster series is to bring awareness to fertility preservation thru the eyes and words of a young adult survivor and call to action to both patients and their health care team to integrate this important discussion into practice.

Project: Multi-Page Layout


A TYPE OF COOKBOOK - The objective of this project was to explore the conceptual and expressive approaches to information design and simultaneously learn the importance of consistency and continuity in a multi-page format. All expressive type images were limited to type, punctuation or type ornaments. The result was a fun project using letterforms for both color and rendering.

Project: Personal Branding

The logo for my personal identity system came from exploring themes that were personally influencial in my life. My maternal grandmother "Nanay" has always been a moral compass for me and now also serves as a creative compass. In any creative endeavor, there is always the element of sensitivity and vulnerability associated with produced work. While I try to keep an objective perspective on the design problem at hand, I often ask myself, "What would Nanay say?" In the end, she would ask me if whether the work I produced was authentic and gave "voice". The pineapple integrated into the logo mark illustrates that my grandmother is always in my heart and her voice in my creative expression.
